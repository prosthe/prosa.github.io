Programs & Activities of PROSA

Advocacy & Student Rights: PROSA frequently raises issues affecting students — for example, they criticized the University of Liberia administration on student welfare matters, showing up at press events to call for more transparency. They have publicly condemned policies or decisions they see as harmful to students. For instance, they strongly criticized the launch of PhD programs at UL, arguing that more fundamental student needs were not being addressed. They also push for financial transparency, especially in how student fees are used — e.g., they raised concerns over fees for student ID cards. National & Social Justice Activism: PROSA doesn’t just focus on campus issues — they engage in national-level advocacy. For example, they have organized (or planned) marches to protest corruption. Analyst Liberia They also mobilize around broader social issues: in July 2025, they scheduled a nationwide march against sexual and gender-based violence (SGBV). Their activism extends to holding the government accountable. In one statement, they condemned alleged bribery by high-level officials and called out corruption in national institutions. Analyst Liberia. Press Engagement & Media Presence: PROSA often holds press conferences to communicate their positions both on student-specific issues and national matters. The New Republic Liberia: They also use social media (e.g., Facebook) to mobilize support, announce activities, and articulate their mission. Political Representation on Campus: PROSA is formally recognized as a campus-based political party at the University of Liberia. They participate in student governance, representing student interests and pushing for reforms. Confronting Security/Police Issues: They have also publicly challenged security forces. For example, PROSA “growled” at the Liberia National Police for what they described as harsh handling of students. Solidarity & Mobilization: PROSA organizes solidarity actions. One such activity is their march for fire victims — they called for a “Long March” to draw attention to fire disaster victims and to urge government and humanitarian interventions. They also tie their marches to broader political mobilization (“Avant-garde party” framing), indicating they see themselves not just as a student group but as a political movement. Analyst Liberia

Our Story

2015

Foundation

PROSA was founded by a group of student activists at Central University to address growing concerns about educational inequality.

2015

National Expansion

Expanded to 15 universities across the country, establishing our first national coordinating committee.

2015

Major Campaigns

Successfully led the "Fund Our Future" campaign, securing $50,000 in additional student funding.

2015

Present Day

Now active in 10 universities, continuing to fight for student rights and progressive values.
